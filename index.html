graph-based dynamic route planning ...

july to september, 2018

While working for the University of Southern California as a research assistant, I worked with a post graduate who had gotten a research grant from Huawei. We were tasked with researching and developing a way to quickly and optimally match drivers to multi-restaurant food delivery orders. I co-authored the paper titled “Graph-based Dynamic Route Planning for Collective Customer Matching”. My time at USC was spent tackling conceptual problems with my advisor and programing the experiments in python.

aqustic

april to june, 2017

My Introduction to Software Engineering class tasked our team of four with developing a project using Scrum development practices. I took on the role of project leader on our team and came up with the idea for aqusitc. Aqustic is a web application that allowed party-goers to collaboratively select music by adding songs to queue and liking/disliking songs so that everyone has a say in choice of music. We developed our web app using Node.js, express, and the Spotify API.

kyphs

march, 2017

During the hackathon ‘Hacktech’, my team and I created a 3D printed set of sensors placed that was made to line the spine of the user. From the device, we could gather data that would be transmitted onto a web application. This device was intended to give information to people who have poor posture and could be used for correctional and medical use. Half the team worked on created the device and getting data where the other half of the team worked on the web application. My teammate and I used Node.js, HTML, and CSS to create a site that displayed the data from the device in a clear, user-friendly manner.
